Finance Compliance - Regulatory Requirements and Standards

Financial services AI must satisfy anti-discrimination laws, consumer protection standards, and model risk management frameworks

Financial institutions deploying AI face comprehensive regulatory requirements across multiple domains. Fair lending and anti-discrimination laws including the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (ECOA) and Fair Housing Act prohibit discrimination based on protected characteristics in credit decisions. AI systems must be validated to ensure they don't produce disparate impact on protected groups, even when those characteristics aren't explicitly used as inputs. Consumer protection regulations enforced by agencies including the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) prohibit unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices (UDAP). AI systems must provide clear disclosures, accurate information, and fair treatment. Model risk management guidance from banking regulators requires financial institutions to validate AI models, document their development and performance, implement effective challenge processes, and maintain ongoing monitoring. Explainability requirements mandate that institutions explain adverse actions such as credit denials, requiring AI systems to provide specific, actionable reasons. Data privacy and security regulations including GDPR (for EU operations) and various state privacy laws require protecting customer information and providing transparency about data use.
