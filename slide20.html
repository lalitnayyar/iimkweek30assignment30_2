Continuous Monitoring - Key Performance Indicators and Metrics

Effective AI monitoring requires comprehensive KPIs tracking model performance, fairness, compliance status, and operational health

Organizations must establish and track Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) that provide comprehensive visibility into AI system health and compliance. Model performance metrics include accuracy, precision, recall, F1 score, and area under the ROC curve, tracked over time to detect degradation. Fairness metrics measure outcomes across demographic groups using statistical parity difference, equal opportunity difference, average odds difference, and disparate impact ratios, ensuring equitable treatment. Compliance status indicators track adherence to regulatory requirements, policy violations, control effectiveness, and audit findings. Data quality metrics monitor completeness, accuracy, consistency, and timeliness of input data, as poor data quality undermines model reliability. Drift detection identifies when input data distributions change (data drift) or when relationships between inputs and outputs shift (concept drift), both of which can degrade model performance and require retraining. Operational metrics track system availability, response times, resource utilization, and error rates. Business impact measures connect AI performance to organizational outcomes such as decision quality, productivity gains, cost savings, and customer satisfaction.
