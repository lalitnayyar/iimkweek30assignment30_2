Slide 12: Ethical Compliance Frameworks - AI Fairness 360 Toolkit

IBM's AI Fairness 360 provides comprehensive open-source tools for detecting and mitigating bias throughout the AI lifecycle

AI Fairness 360 represents a leading example of practical tools that translate fairness research into operational practice. This extensible open-source toolkit, now maintained by the Linux Foundation AI, helps organizations examine, report, and mitigate discrimination in machine learning models across finance, healthcare, human capital management, and education sectors. The toolkit provides 10 state-of-the-art bias mitigation algorithms including optimized preprocessing, reweighing, adversarial debiasing, reject option classification, disparate impact remover, learning fair representations, equalized odds post-processing, and meta-fair classifiers. Over 70 fairness metrics quantify individual and group fairness through measures including statistical parity difference, equal opportunity difference, average odds difference, disparate impact, Theil index, and various distance metrics. Industrial application tutorials demonstrate real-world use cases in credit scoring and medical expenditure prediction, offering data scientists practical guidance for implementation. Available in both Python and R, the toolkit enables organizations to integrate fairness considerations into existing ML workflows.
