GDPR establishes comprehensive data protection requirements that fundamentally shape AI development and deployment in Europe

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), enacted by the European Union in 2008, creates binding requirements for AI systems processing personal data of EU citizens. These requirements are foundational for any multinational corporation operating in or dealing with data from the EU.

Key GDPR Requirements for AI Systems:

Lawful basis for processing demands explicit consent that is willingly provided, specific, informed, and unequivocal, or legitimate interest that balances organizational needs against individual rights. AI systems must be able to demonstrate a clear legal ground for every data processing activity.

Data minimization and purpose limitation require using only the minimum necessary data for specified purposes, prohibiting repurposing without additional consent. This principle directly impacts AI training data, mandating that models are trained on the least amount of personal data required to achieve the stated objective.

Anonymization and pseudonymization protect privacy while enabling AI to derive insights from large datasets—anonymization permanently prevents identification, while pseudonymization replaces identifiers with pseudonyms. These techniques are crucial for developing and testing AI models with sensitive data in a compliant manner.

Security and accountability mandate integrated security practices to prevent breaches, comprehensive records of data processing activities, Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing, and data protection by design and by default. DPIAs are particularly relevant for AI, as they force a systematic assessment of privacy risks before deployment.

Individual rights include access to personal data, portability to other services, explanation of automated decisions, and the right to be forgotten through complete data erasure. The "right to explanation" for automated decisions (Article 22) is a cornerstone of GDPR's impact on AI, requiring transparency in algorithmic decision-making.
