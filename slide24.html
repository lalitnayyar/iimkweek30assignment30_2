Slide 24: Strategic Recommendations - Implementation Roadmap

Phased implementation enables organizations to build AI governance capabilities progressively while delivering early value

Organizations should adopt a phased approach to implementing AI governance frameworks. This strategy manages risk, builds organizational capabilities progressively, and demonstrates value that sustains executive support. The roadmap is structured into four distinct phases:

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)

Establishes governance structures, conducts current state assessment of existing AI systems and practices, defines ethical principles and policies, forms cross-functional governance teams, and selects initial pilot use cases in one sector.

Phase 2: Pilot Implementation (Months 4-6)

Implements bias mitigation and explainability tools in pilot use cases, develops monitoring dashboards and KPIs, conducts Data Protection Impact Assessments, establishes documentation standards, and validates approaches with regulators where appropriate.

Phase 3: Expansion (Months 7-12)

Scales successful approaches across additional use cases and sectors, integrates governance into standard AI development processes, implements comprehensive monitoring infrastructure, conducts organization-wide training on responsible AI, and establishes regular reporting to leadership and board.

Phase 4: Optimization (Months 13-18)

Refines processes based on lessons learned, enhances automation of monitoring and compliance checking, deepens integration with enterprise risk management, benchmarks against industry best practices, and pursues relevant certifications or attestations.
