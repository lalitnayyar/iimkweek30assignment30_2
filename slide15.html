FDA's evolving regulatory approach balances AI innovation with patient safety through lifecycle management and transparency requirements

The Food and Drug Administration regulates AI-enabled medical devices through premarket pathways including 510(k) clearance, De Novo classification, and premarket approval. However, the FDA's traditional paradigm was not designed for adaptive AI/ML technologies that learn and evolve after deployment, creating regulatory challenges.

The AI/ML Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) Action Plan (January 2021) established a framework for addressing these challenges through predetermined change control plans that allow certain modifications without new premarket review.

Good Machine Learning Practice for Medical Device Development (October 2021) provides guiding principles for data quality, feature engineering, training, and performance monitoring. Transparency for ML-Enabled Medical Devices (June 2024) emphasizes the need for clear communication about how AI systems function, their limitations, and appropriate use contexts.

The Draft Guidance on Lifecycle Management (January 2025) proposes comprehensive recommendations for marketing submissions, focusing on transparency, bias mitigation, and safety across the Total Product Life Cycle (TPLC).

Organizations developing AI medical devices must implement robust quality management systems, conduct thorough validation studies, and maintain ongoing surveillance.
