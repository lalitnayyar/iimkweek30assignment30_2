Slide 7: Bias Mitigation Strategies - Three-Stage Intervention Framework

Effective bias mitigation requires interventions at pre-processing, in-processing, and post-processing stages of algorithm development

The research community has developed a comprehensive three-stage framework for reducing algorithmic bias. Organizations should implement interventions at all three stages for comprehensive bias mitigation.

Pre-processing Interventions

Address bias in training data before model development. This stage focuses on cleaning and balancing the input data to ensure fair representation.

  • Data augmentation to increase representation of underrepresented groups.
  • Reweighing samples to balance demographic distributions.
  • Disparate impact removal to eliminate discriminatory patterns from input data.
In-processing Interventions

Modify the learning algorithm itself to promote fairness during the model training phase. This ensures the model learns fair decision boundaries.

  • Adversarial debiasing that trains models to make accurate predictions while preventing discrimination.
  • Regularization techniques that penalize unfair outcomes.
  • Fairness constraints that explicitly require equitable treatment across groups.
Post-processing Interventions

Adjust model outputs to achieve fairness goals after the model has made a prediction. This is a final check and correction mechanism.

  • Reject option classification that reconsiders borderline decisions for disadvantaged groups.
  • Equalized odds adjustments that balance error rates across demographics.
  • Calibration techniques that ensure consistent prediction accuracy.
