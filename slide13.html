Slide 13: Comprehensive Compliance Frameworks

Global AI governance frameworks provide complementary approaches to ensuring ethical and responsible AI development

Beyond GDPR and specialized tools, organizations should reference multiple complementary frameworks. The convergence of these frameworks around core principles—transparency, fairness, accountability, privacy, and security—enables organizations to develop coherent governance approaches that satisfy diverse regulatory requirements. Organizations operating across multiple jurisdictions should implement frameworks that meet the most stringent applicable standards while maintaining operational efficiency.

Key Global Frameworks and Standards

OECD AI Principles

Establish internationally recognized guidelines for trustworthy AI including human-centered values, transparency, robustness, accountability, and respect for human rights.

UNESCO Recommendation on AI Ethics

Provides comprehensive guidance on ethical considerations in AI development and deployment, focusing on human rights and fundamental freedoms.

NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF)

Offers a structured approach to identifying, assessing, and mitigating AI-related risks across the system lifecycle, focusing on governance, mapping, measuring, and managing.

ISO 42001 (AI Management Systems)

An international standard for AI management systems, enabling organizations to demonstrate systematic approaches to AI governance, risk management, and compliance.

IEEE 7000 Series Standards

Addresses ethical considerations in system design, ensuring values are embedded from the earliest stages of technology development.
